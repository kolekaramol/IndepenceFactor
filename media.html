Once King Yadu (ancestor of Krishna) met Avadhut Dattatreya on the way. Impressed by his enigmatic carefreeness, the King was drawn towards him. He humbly prostrated before Dattatreya and asked, 'Sir, may I know how is it that you seem to be so happy? What is the source of your happiness, though you appear to be like a beggar? Who are you? May I know your whereabouts and a little of your history?

Dattatreya did not say who he was. He merely said, 'I am happy because of what I am, not because of what I have"!
